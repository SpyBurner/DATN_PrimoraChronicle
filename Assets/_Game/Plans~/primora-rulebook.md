# Primora Chronicle — Implementation Fact List

This document is the authoritative source for implementing Primora Chronicle. Every statement is a positive fact. The implementor builds only what is listed here.

---

## 1. Board

- The board is a finite hexagonal grid using an axial `(n, p, q)` offset coordinate system.
- The targeted tile is the `(0, 0)` pivot for all AOE and range calculations.
- Each hex tile holds a maximum of 1 unit at a time.
- Movement or knockback that reaches the board boundary stops the unit at the outermost hex.
- Each player owns one designated corner tile called the Deploy Area.
- The Deploy Area tile is permanently immune to all environmental tile effects.
- At board clear, the Deploy Area tile is fully cleared of all units and tile effects before the next Main Phase begins.
- Any unit destroyed by Deploy Area clearing goes to the Discard Pile.

---

## 2. Factions

- Three factions exist: Hollow Procession, Verdant Dominion, Ashen Legion.
- A card's faction tag is narrative only. It places no restriction on deck building.
- A player is immune to the negative effects of Lingering tile effects generated by their own faction's cards.

---

## 3. Cards & Deck Construction

### Deck Composition
- A valid deck contains exactly 1 Champion card and exactly 20 support cards.
- There is no Mana Cost system. The `mana_cost` field is ignored at runtime.

### Card Types
- **Champion:** The player's avatar. One per deck. Always available during the Main Phase.
- **Troop:** A deployable unit. Serves as the base of a fusion.
- **EquipSpell:** Fused onto a unit during the Main Phase to grant skills or status effects permanently.
- **MainPhaseSpell:** Played from hand during the Main Phase. Queued for the Pre-Combat Spell Clash.

### Rarity
- **Common:** Freely collectable. No copy limit per deck.
- **Champion rarity, `is_summonable: true`:** Grantable by a Champion's `grants_cards` list. Collectible and addable to a deck.
- **Champion rarity, `is_summonable: false`:** An engine-only stat reference. Loaded by the engine as unit data only. Never enters a player's hand, deck, or fusion UI. Exists on the board only when spawned by a skill.

### Granted Cards
- At match start, a Champion's `grants_cards` entries are shuffled directly into the player's 20-card deck.
- The final deck size equals 20 plus the total quantity of all granted cards.

---

## 4. Fusion

- A player assembles exactly 1 unit per turn during the Main Phase.
- A unit has exactly 4 fusion slots.
- If the base Troop has an innate skill (`grants_skill`), that skill occupies 1 of the 4 slots automatically.
- Each EquipSpell card fused onto the unit occupies exactly 1 slot.
- Duplicate EquipSpell cards are allowed in the same fusion.
- A unit may be deployed with fewer than 4 EquipSpells fused (including zero).
- The fully assembled unit is placed on the player's Deploy Area tile.
- All cards used in a fusion (the Troop card and all fused EquipSpells) go to the Discard Pile at the end of the Combat Phase, regardless of whether the unit survived or died.

---

## 5. Match Flow

### Start Phase
- The player selects a pre-built deck.
- If the player does not confirm in time, their most recently played deck is auto-selected.
- The Champion's granted cards are shuffled into the deck.
- The player's HP is set to their Champion's `hp` value.
- The player is dealt an opening hand of 6 cards using Draw Phase logic.

### Main Phase
- The player assembles and deploys up to 1 unit (optional).
- The player may play any number of MainPhaseSpell cards from hand. Each is queued for the Spell Clash.
- The board state from the previous Combat Phase is active during the Main Phase. Persistent NPCs and lingering tile effects are present.
- Queued MainPhaseSpells interact with the live board state when they eventually resolve.

### Combat Phase
The Combat Phase runs in this order:

**Step 1 — Pre-Combat Spell Clash**
- All queued MainPhaseSpells resolve before any unit acts.
- Resolve order: sorted by the Speed of the unit each player is deploying this turn, highest Speed first.
- A player deploying no unit this turn resolves their spell last.
- Speed ties: lowest HP resolves first. Remaining ties: coin toss.
- The Spell Clash uses the full Aggregate → Intercept → Commit modifier pipeline.
- Tile-manipulation spells targeting the same tile are mutex-locked: the first player to confirm their target locks those tiles until the effect resolves. The second spell then resolves on the updated board state.
- If a player's HP reaches 0 during the Spell Clash, they are immediately eliminated. Their unit for this turn is not deployed.

**Step 2 — Build Action Queue**
- All units on the board are sorted into an Action Queue by Speed, highest first.
- Speed ties: lowest HP goes first. Remaining ties: coin toss.
- Units spawned by skills mid-combat are appended to the end of the current Action Queue.

**Step 3 — Unit Turns**
- Each unit in queue order takes its turn.
- At the start of a unit's turn, all its skill cooldowns tick down by 1.
- On its turn, a unit may move up to its hex movement range AND perform one action (Normal Attack or one Active Skill).
- Movement and action may occur in any order within the unit's turn.
- A unit may choose not to move, not to act, or both.
- Movement paths only through empty tiles.
- Normal attacks and AOE skills affect only enemy tiles. Allied tiles are ignored automatically.
- Skills with `ignore_friendly_fire: true` affect both allied and enemy tiles.
- Skills with `ignore_pathfinding: true` move the unit directly to the destination tile without checking intermediate tiles. Only the destination tile must be valid.
- Using a jump skill (`ignore_pathfinding: true`) constitutes the unit's action for that turn. The unit does not receive an additional separate movement.

**Step 4 — Death & Death Anchor**
- When a unit's HP reaches 0, it is destroyed immediately.
- The destroyed unit's `death_anchor` value is immediately subtracted from the owning player's HP.
- If a player's HP reaches 0 at any point during combat, they are immediately eliminated.
- All units belonging to an eliminated player are destroyed at the moment of elimination.
- Elimination is checked continuously, not at end of phase.

**Step 5 — Board Clear**
- Board clear triggers when only one player's units remain on the board.
- All player-deployed units on the board (surviving or destroyed) go to the Discard Pile.
- Persistent NPCs remain on the board and carry into the next Main Phase.
- Lingering tile effects (Corrupted, Seeded, Melting) remain on their tiles.
- All non-lingering tile effects and non-persistent unit buffs/debuffs are removed.
- The Deploy Area tile is forcibly cleared of any unit or effect.

### Draw Phase
- The player is shown 2 newly drawn cards from their deck.
- The player uses a drag-and-drop UI to manage their full hand (current hand + newly drawn cards).
- The player may keep any combination of cards up to a maximum hand size of 6.
- Cards not kept are placed in the Discard Pile.
- If the deck is empty when a draw is required, the Discard Pile is immediately shuffled into a new deck, then the draw proceeds.

### Win Condition
- The last player with HP above 0 is the winner.
- If multiple players' HP reaches 0 at the exact same moment, all of those players receive a Loss, an XP penalty, and reduced Gold.
- If the match time limit of 1 hour expires, the player with the highest remaining HP wins.
- If multiple players are tied for highest HP at time expiry, all tied players receive a Loss.

---

## 6. AI-Controlled Units

- Units spawned by skill effects are AI-controlled.
- AI units move toward the closest enemy unit each turn.
- AI units execute a random valid attack or skill against the closest enemy.
- AI units are appended to the end of the Action Queue when spawned mid-combat.
- AI units follow all the same rules as player-controlled units (speed ordering, cooldowns, pathing, etc.).

---

## 7. Persistent NPCs

- Persistent NPCs are units with `is_summonable: false`. They are spawned exclusively by skills.
- Persistent NPCs survive board clear and remain on the board across combat cycles.
- Persistent NPC skill cooldowns carry over between combat cycles.
- Persistent NPCs are AI-controlled at all times.
- Persistent NPCs have fixed stats and skills set at spawn. They receive no fusions.
- Persistent NPCs obey the Deploy Area clearing rule. If occupying the Deploy Area at board clear, they are destroyed and go to the Discard Pile.

---

## 8. The Verdant Evolution Chain

- The evolution chain is: Seedling → Sapling → Young Treant → Thorn Colossus.
- All four forms are persistent NPCs (`is_summonable: false`).
- A Seedling is spawned by `skill_summon_seedling` on a random empty tile adjacent to the activating unit.
- At the start of a Seedling's turn, if it is standing on a Seeded tile, it gains 1 Growth Stack.
- When a unit in the chain accumulates 4 Growth Stacks, it immediately evolves to the next form.
- Upon evolution, all Growth Stacks are cleared.
- The evolved unit retains its board position and all tile effects currently on that tile.
- Thorn Colossus is the final form. Accumulating 4 Growth Stacks on a Thorn Colossus has no further evolution effect.

---

## 9. Damage & The Modifier Pipeline

- All damage is applied directly to HP. There is no base armor.
- Before any action resolves, the system runs three passes in strict order:
  1. **Aggregate Pass:** All active stat changes triggered by the action are gathered into a temporary buffer.
  2. **Intercept Pass:** Defensive status effects evaluate the buffer and cancel or modify specific incoming values.
  3. **Commit Pass:** The final resolved values are written to actual unit stats.
- Within each pass, Tile effects are evaluated before Unit effects.

---

## 10. Targeting

- All targeting is tile-based. Targeting a unit means targeting the tile that unit occupies.
- `target_condition` is a bitmask: `Enemy = 1`, `Ally = 2`, `EmptyTile = 4`.
- Bitmask flags combine with bitwise OR (e.g., `3` = Enemy or Ally, `7` = any tile).
- `target_condition: 0` means self-only. No tile selection is made; the effect applies to the acting unit's own tile.

---

## 11. Tile Effects

### Lingering Effects (Corrupted, Seeded, Melting)
- Lingering effects persist through board clear into the next cycle.
- Only one Lingering effect occupies a tile at a time.
- Applying a new Lingering effect to a tile that holds a different Lingering effect replaces the old one entirely.
- Applying the same Lingering effect to a tile that already holds it refreshes or stacks according to its own behavior.
- The owning player's units are immune to the negative effects of their own faction's Lingering effects.

### Standard Tile Effects
- Standard tile effects (e.g., Ash Cloud, Scorching Ground, Banner of Cinders) persist only for the current combat cycle and are cleared at board clear.
- Multiple standard tile effects stack on the same tile normally.
- Standard tile effects are removed by specific Purify card effects.

### Decay (Special Case)
- Decay is a Unit-type effect that travels with the afflicted unit when it moves.
- When a unit with Decay stands on a Corrupted tile, the Corrupted damage is increased by 1 per Decay stack on that unit.

---

## 12. Stat Modification Rules

- Any effect that modifies Max HP also modifies current HP by the same delta.
- If an effect increases Max HP, current HP increases by the same amount.
- If an effect decreases Max HP and current HP would exceed the new Max HP, current HP is set equal to the new Max HP.
- Status effects that modify stats are applied permanently to the unit for the duration of the status effect.
- The `ignore_friendly_fire: true` flag on a skill is the only mechanism that permits a skill to affect allied units.
- The `ignore_pathfinding: true` flag on a skill is the only mechanism that permits movement without checking intermediate tiles.

---

## 13. Skill Cooldowns

- A skill's cooldown ticks down by 1 at the start of the owning unit's turn.
- A skill is usable when its cooldown value reaches 0.
- Skills with `one_time: true` are permanently disabled after their first use in the match.
- Persistent NPC cooldowns carry across board clear cycles.

---

## 14. Hand & Deck Management

- Maximum hand size is 6 cards at any time.
- The Discard Pile is the single destination for all discarded, consumed, and spent cards.
- When a draw is needed and the deck is empty, the Discard Pile is shuffled immediately into a new deck.
- Cards in the Discard Pile are indistinguishable from one another in terms of draw probability after reshuffling.

---

## 15. Behavior System

- Each skill references a `skill_behavior_id` that maps to a Scriptable Object defining its full execution logic.
- Each status effect references a `status_effect_behavior_id` that maps to a Scriptable Object defining its per-turn logic.
- Each MainPhaseSpell references a `main_phase_spell_behavior_id` that maps to a Scriptable Object defining its resolution logic.
- Behavior IDs are treated as opaque strings by the engine. Validation occurs at asset load time.
- All logic specific to a behavior (e.g., conditional Champion HP penalty in Humanity Rejection) lives inside the behavior Scriptable Object. The data schema expresses no conditional logic directly.

---

## 16. Display Rules

- Active skills have a `display_pattern` field used to preview the effect area while the player selects a target tile.
- Passive skills do not have a `display_pattern`. They are triggered by the engine with no player targeting step.
